üìñ Historical Context

Fort Wolters Primary Helicopter Training School

Fort Wolters served as the U.S. Army's primary helicopter training facility from 1956 to 1973, training over 40,000 helicopter pilots during its operational period. Located near Mineral Wells, Texas, the base played a crucial role in developing the Army's rotary-wing aviation capabilities during the Vietnam War era.

The Hughes TH-55A Osage

The Hughes TH-55A Osage was introduced to Fort Wolters in 1965, replacing earlier training aircraft. This two-seat, piston-engine helicopter became the primary trainer for Army aviators. Class 65-9WA represents one of the first classes to complete training on this aircraft, marking a significant transition in Army aviation training methodology.

Class 65-9WA Significance

As indicated by the inscription, this lighter was presented to a member of the first graduating class to use the Hughes TH-55A helicopter. The "65-9" designation indicates graduation in 1965, while "WA" denotes the specific class section. This makes the artifact particularly significant as it commemorates a milestone in Army aviation training history.

Warrant Officer Candidate (WOC) Program

The recipient, WOC Arthur P. Lancy Jr., was part of the Warrant Officer Candidate program, which trained enlisted soldiers and junior officers to become Army aviators. Upon graduation, candidates received their wings and were commissioned as Warrant Officers.
